about Ibrahim Tuqan

Tuqan was born in Nablus in 1905. He attended the al-Rashadiyya al-Gharbiyya School in western Nablus for his primary education, then St. George's School in Jerusalem for his secondary education.

Poetry

Tuqan's poetry is characterized by its nationalistic themes and its call for Arab unity and resistance against colonial rule. His most famous poem, "Mawtini" ("My Homeland"), has been adopted as the national anthem of several Arab countries.

family

Tuqan came from a prominent Palestinian family known for their contributions to literature and politics. His brother, Fadwa Tuqan, was also a renowned poet, and his sister, Leila Tuqan, was a notable writer and activist.
